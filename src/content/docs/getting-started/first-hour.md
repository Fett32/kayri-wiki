The core loop of the pack, once you're in-world.

## Pokémon basics

- Send out your first party member with **R**. Recall is also R.
- **M** opens the Pokémon Summary (stats, moves, IVs).
- Pokémon heal at **Healing Machines** — craft one, or find them in Pokémon Centers around the world.
- **Pokémon Centers** also act as village-like social spots with traders.

## The world is different

- **Overworld hostile mobs are gone.** No creepers, zombies, skeletons, spiders. Pokémon fill the ecosystem.
- **Wild Pokémon can attack you** (Fight or Flight) — press **G** to battle back.
- Ender Dragon and Wither exist; the rest of the vanilla mobs are disabled.
- **Temperature matters** — deserts will cook you, snowy biomes will freeze you. Leather = warm, iron = cool, drink water in heat. See [Temperature Survival](/base/temperature-survival/) for details.

## Travel & inventory

- **Xaero minimap** (top-right corner) + full map on **K**. Waypoints with **`,`**.
- **Traveler's Backpack** on **J** — craftable extra inventory you wear.
- **Waystones** exist across the world — expensive to craft but free to use. Feel free to explore to find a base location.

## Money & items

- **Pokédollars** drop from trainer battles and merchant villagers. Buy Poké Balls, TMs, and items.
- **TMs/TRs** teach moves — found in loot chests and crafted.
- Pokémon drop useful items when defeated — Mareep drops wool, Miltank drops beef, Koffing drops gunpowder. **Vanilla materials come from Pokémon now.** See the [full drops lookup](/reference/drops-lookup/).
- Key Nether/End gap drops (since those mobs are gone): **Blaze Rods** from Magmar / Magmortar, **Shulker Shells** from Cloyster / Omastar, **Wither Skeleton Skulls** from Marowak. Ender Pearls and Blaze Powder drop from many wild Pokémon by default.

## Death

- Keep-Inventory is off by default, but **Lenient Death** keeps your stuff at the death spot much longer than vanilla. Go retrieve it.
- You keep **equipment, armor, and tools** on death. Poké Balls and most items are not kept — go back for them.

## Early goals

1. Pick a starter (**M**), from any generation.
2. Find apricorns (colored trees) and craft Poké Balls with copper bars.
3. Type in the bar at the bottom of your inventory to search items. Click an item on the right to see its recipe.
4. Set up a small base near a village or Pokémon Center so you have healing nearby. You can craft machines later.
5. Use waystones to scout a good base location once you have the means.
